A week without a racket
A coach in Valencia had a teenager who hit the ball as hard as anyone alive — and missed twice as often. He took the kid's racket away for a week. Made him shadow-swing in a mirror, catching the racket over his shoulder, again and again. "You don't have a forehand problem," he told him. "You have a finish problem — and a clean finish means everything before it was right." The kid stopped trying to murder the ball and started holding his finish. The pace didn't drop. The errors did.
